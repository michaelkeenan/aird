  • There are several well-funded companies explicitly trying to build AGI, most notably: OpenAI and DeepMind. The field is growing - for example, game developer John Carmack (who does not have notable AI experience) has founded an AGI company with an initial investment of $20M.
  • As consumers, we usually prefer more general systems. For example:
    • People prefer smartphones with many capabilities over simple phones
    • Google Maps and Google Calendar are good separately. But they are better if they can be combined. And they would be even better if they had the capability, without prompt, to automatically call an Uber and figure out your travel directions
    • Desktop PC operating systems like MacOS and Windows initially only had a small range of software available, and they were only capable of interfacing with a small number of hardware devices. Over the years, the options in both hardware and software increased enormously.
  • AGI has important advantages over an assembly of task-based AIs.
